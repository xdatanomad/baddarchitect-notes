# Content Quality Checklist

Use this before promoting raw notes into publishable content.

- The reader is clearly defined.
- The page answers one primary question or job-to-be-done.
- The thesis appears early.
- Claims are separated into experience, opinion, and cited facts.
- External statistics or modern company claims have sources.
- The content includes practical next steps, examples, or decision criteria.
- The tone is direct, useful, and non-hype.
- Private personal context from `MEMORY.md` is not included.
- The page has a proposed title, description, slug, and internal links.
- The page fits one content pillar or is explicitly marked as exploratory.
